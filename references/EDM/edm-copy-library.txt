Dig the well before you're thirsty

This Plastic Free July, each of us at Memobottle is committed to cutting out one
single-use item. How? By keeping our reusables at the ready.

Using the power of preparation, we're snipping off 'single-use' at the source, and
setting ourselves up for success.

It looks different for everyone, but it's really as simple as keeping a small buffer
of reusable cups or grocery bags in your car.

In making this 1 change, over 1 month, we aim to form a new habit that will last
a lifetime.

Change doesn't happen overnight, so we hope you'll join us in taking this small step
towards a more reusable lifestyle.

NEED SOME INSPIRATION? HERE'S WHAT WE'RE DOING.

REUSABLE COFFEE CUPS AT THE READY
We're caffeinated and caring for the environment by pre-packing a couple coffee cups
each day. This way they're always ready to go for our morning pick-me-ups... and
we've got an office buddy accountability system going strong. That's that me, espresso.

WASTE-FREE FOOD STORAGE
Our leftover pasta salad? Kept fresh for tomorrow's lunch using beeswax wrap, reusable
containers, and glass jars instead of plastic wrap. Tastes better the next day.

ZERO-WASTE GROCERY SHOPPING
We're shopping at bulk food stores for everything from rice, to dried fruit and nuts,
to home cleaning supplies. By using our own jars and containers, and picking up the
exact quantities we need, we're reducing our packaging and food waste. A win-win.

LATHER, RINSE, REPEAT
We're switching to bar soaps to reduce our plastic waste. Lavender? Lemon myrtle?
Sign us up. Or, if you prefer liquid shower gels, try filling up your bottles at
bulk food stores instead.
