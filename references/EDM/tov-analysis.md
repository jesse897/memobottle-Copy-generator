# memobottle Email TOV Analysis
**Based on:** Klaviyo campaigns, Nov–Dec 2025
**Primary sources:** Butter Yellow launches, New SS Colours series, Holiday Giveaway, Survey emails
**Secondary (sales-period, weighted less):** BFCM series, Christmas Gifting, Boxing Day

---

## Overall Verdict

The brand voice is at its best when writing for product and colour launches. The Butter Yellow and SS Colours series show a team that genuinely understands the voice — playful without being try-hard, confident without overstating. The weaknesses are specific and fixable: em dashes slipping through consistently, some over-written descriptive passages, underuse of key content pillars, and utility emails (surveys, thank-yous) that feel generic.

---

## What's Working Well

### Hero headlines are nailing the brief
The colour launch heroes are the strongest copy in the set:
- "NOW SERVING: BUTTER YELLOW"
- "DRINK PINK"
- "NUTS ABOUT PISTACHIO"
- "'TIS THE SEASON TO BE BUTTERY"

Short, punchy, playful, and colour-matched in energy — exactly what the TOV calls for. The food metaphor thread running through Butter Yellow ("anything but cookie-cutter", "serving up", "explore the spread", "melts away for good", "festive favourites", "our menu") is the best example of earned dry humour in the entire set. This is the brand at its best.

### Colour personality is being matched correctly
The TOV says to match the energy of the colour. This is being executed well:
- Pink Salt gets romantic, wanderer-focused copy ("the romantics, and the curious")
- Pistachio gets modernist/contrast language ("celebrates the beauty of contrasts, bold, modernist design")
- Butter Yellow gets warmth and lifestyle copy ("slow mornings and gentle sunshine")

### Australian/British English is consistent
"colour", "colourway", "favourite", "flavour" — all correct throughout.

### B Corp credentials are handled with restraint
"BETTER PRODUCTS / BIGGER PURPOSE" in the footer is exactly right — understated, not self-congratulatory. Consistent across templates.

### Product naming is mostly correct
"the memobottle", "the Stainless Steel memobottle" — correctly lowercase and framed. No instances of "our bottle" in the primary emails.

## Issues & Flags

### 1. Em dashes — used repeatedly in body copy
**Rule: no em dashes. Use a spaced dash ( - ) or rewrite.**

Found in body copy images (not just subject lines):
- Butter Yellow EDM 1: "Flat and space-saving design that allows for a Tetris-style fit in your bag — making the most of the space you have"
- Butter Yellow EDM 1: "Butter Yellow's rich, decadent tone radiates warmth and comfort — reminiscent of slow mornings and gentle sunshine"
- Subject lines in the sales period also use them ("15% off sitewide continues — limited time only!", "Free shipping on your Christmas order* — 5 days only")

This is the most consistent technical violation across the set. It's appearing in the primary (non-sales) emails, which means it's a habit rather than an oversight.

### 2. Over-written descriptive passages
Some colour description copy is running longer than it needs to. The TOV benchmark is max 2-3 sentences — often just one. The Pistachio description nails it (one sentence). Pink Salt's "DESIGNED FOR YOU" section drifts:

> "Inviting a splash of creative, playful charm into your day, Pink Salt is designed for life in motion, and for those who go with the flow: the wanderers, the romantics, and the curious."

This is trying a bit hard. It could be: "For the wanderers, the romantics, and the curious."

### 3. Weak superlatives in gifting copy
Butter Yellow EDM 2: "the memobottle is the perfect thoughtful gift for those who appreciate something out-of-the-box."

"Perfect" and "out-of-the-box" are both soft clichés. The TOV says confidence is understated — a stronger line might lean into the design angle rather than a gifting trope.

### 4. Survey and utility emails feel generic
The survey emails ("WE WANT TO HEAR FROM YOU") are clean visually but the copy has no brand personality. There's an opportunity to write these in voice — a short, dry opener would go a long way. At the moment they read like any DTC brand's survey email.

### 5. Giveaway copy uses urgency clichés
The Holiday Giveaway EDM 3 uses "LAST CHANCE", "DON'T MISS THIS", and "THE TRIP OF A LIFETIME" — all standard urgency phrases that any brand could write. The body copy is functional but there's no memobottle voice in it.

## Underused Pillars

These are core TOV content pillars that are largely absent from the email set:

**Form factor** — The flat/fits-anywhere design angle barely appears. It gets one mention in the Butter Yellow launch (the Tetris-fit line) but isn't being used as an ongoing hook in subject lines or opening copy. Given it's the brand's most distinctive differentiator, this is a missed opportunity.

**Sustainability** — Beyond the B Corp footer icons, sustainability copy is completely absent from campaign emails. No Water.org mentions, no single-use reduction angle, no matter-of-fact environmental credentials. This is the brand's "why" and it's not showing up at all in EDMs.

**The understated flex** — The TOV has a structure called "the understated flex" (e.g. "Different by design", "Less is more"). This confident, minimal style is absent. Copy is defaulting to longer descriptive passages when a single line would be more powerful.

## Quick Reference: Patterns to Fix

| Issue | Example found | Fix |
|---|---|---|
| Em dashes in body copy | "warmth and comfort — reminiscent of..." | Use " - " or rewrite sentence |
| Em dashes in subject lines | "15% off sitewide continues — limited time" | Replace with " - " or colon |
| Over-written descriptive copy | Pink Salt "DESIGNED FOR YOU" section | Cut to the essential line |
| Generic utility emails | Survey emails | Add a single dry opener in voice |
| Weak gifting superlatives | "the perfect thoughtful gift" | Reframe around design/experience |
| Missing form factor angle | Absent from most EDMs | Weave in as a recurring hook |
| Missing sustainability angle | Absent entirely from campaign emails | Light-touch mention in product EDMs |

## Standout Copy to Use as Internal Benchmarks

These are the best lines from the set — use as reference when briefing or reviewing:

- "NOW SERVING: BUTTER YELLOW" — perfect three-word energy, earns the wordplay
- "Introducing the Stainless Steel bottle in Butter, that's anything but cookie-cutter." — natural dry humour, no forced punchline
- "explore the spread now before this limited-edition colourway melts away for good" — consistent extended metaphor, well-executed
- "A statement-maker made for the keepers of beautiful things." — elevated, design-literate, doesn't overstate
- "DRINK PINK" — two words, earns its place
- "A modern take on the classic blush pink, Pink Salt's ethereal, pastel-like hue beckons a second look." — one sentence, does the job
- "The Stainless Steel memobottle in Pistachio celebrates the beauty of contrasts, with its understated colour palette perfectly balancing its bold, modernist design." — confident, design-led, correct length
